the brave day of tiko and the turtle

Tiko had never meant to be brave. He was small, quick, and much happier thinking about adventures than actually having them. Turtle, on the other hand, was slow, calm, and carried his courage the way he carried his shell—quietly, everywhere.

That morning, the river was wrong. The water rushed faster than usual, tugging at the reeds and whispering trouble. When they heard the cry—help!—both of them froze. A baby otter was stuck on a rock in the middle of the current.
